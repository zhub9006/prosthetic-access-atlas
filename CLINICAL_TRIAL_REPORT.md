## 6. Implications for Access Gaps

1. **Low active recruitment** (17.6%) means most prosthetic innovations remain in completed or unknown status—patients in underserved regions cannot access trials.
2. **Geographic bias**: Most trials are concentrated in academic medical centers in major cities (e.g., Vanderbilt, Charité Berlin, Cleveland Clinic). Rural Appalachia and the Mississippi Delta have zero trial sites.
3. **Industry disinvestment**: Only 16.8% of studies are industry-sponsored, which correlates with fewer resources for patient outreach and travel reimbursement—critical for underserved populations.
4. **Phase gap**: The majority of studies are observational, meaning there is limited evidence for new prosthetic interventions reaching rural communities.

---

## 7. Recommendations

- **Expand trial sites** in underserved ZIP codes (WV, KY, MS Delta) with travel reimbursement programs
- **Leverage telehealth** for prosthetic follow-up in areas without CPO providers
- **Increase industry sponsorship** for prosthetic studies targeting rural populations
- **Track trial access** by proxy measure (distance to nearest trial site, Medicaid enrollment, CPO provider density)
