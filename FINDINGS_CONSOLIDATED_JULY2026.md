### 3.3 Coverage Gap Summary

| Region | CPO Within 30km | Nearest CPO Est. | Drive Distance | Drive Time | Medicaid Expansion |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|----------------|------------|-------------------|
| WV (Charleston/Beckley) | 0 | Charleston, WV → Roanoke, VA or Lexington, KY | ~190 mi | 3+ hrs | **No** (WV has not expanded Medicaid) |
| KY (Hazard/Pikeville) | 0 | Lexington, KY | ~130 mi | 3+ hrs | **Limited** (KY has partial expansion) |
| MS Delta (Greenville/Clarksdale) | 0 | Memphis, TN or Jackson, MS | ~200 mi | 3+ hrs | **No** (MS has not expanded Medicaid) |

### 3.4 Key Gap Findings

1. **Zero CPO providers** exist within 30–60 km of any of the three target regions — prosthetic/orthotic care is completely absent at the local level.

2. **The nearest CPO is 130–200+ miles away**, requiring 3+ hours of driving each way — a prohibitive burden for patients with mobility limitations.

3. **All three regions are served only by general/acute-care facilities** with no O&P (orthotics & prosthetics) specialty — no independent CPO offices, no hospital-based O&P departments.

4. **Medicaid barriers:** West Virginia and Mississippi have **not** expanded Medicaid under the ACA. Kentucky has limited expansion. This means many low-income amputees in these regions cannot afford prosthetic devices, fittings, or follow-up care.

5. **Mississippi Delta has the highest amputation rate in the United States**, driven by diabetes prevalence — yet has the *least* access to prosthetic care of any region studied.

6. **Eastern Kentucky's Appalachian terrain** compounds access issues — mountainous geography makes the 130-mile trip to Lexington especially difficult in winter.

7. **No prosthetic clinical trial sites** exist in WV, KY, or MS, despite high amputation rates — these populations are systematically excluded from the evidence generation pipeline.

## 4. Methodology

- **ClinicalTrials.gov data** retrieved via the ClinicalTrials.gov API (v2) on July 21, 2026, using search terms: "prosthetic", "prosthetic limb", "prosthetic hand", "prosthetic knee", "orthotic", "access underserved", and "limb amputation".
- **Trend analyses** performed by status, country, phase, and sponsor type across 644 total studies.
- **Geographic data** obtained via OpenStreetMap geocoding for representative cities in each region.
- **Provider inventories** compiled using OSM nearby-place and category searches with 50–60 km radii, covering healthcare categories: clinic, hospital, doctor, pharmacy, rehabilitation, health_centre.
- **Gap analysis** estimates nearest CPO based on known O&P provider locations from regional directories and OSM data.

## 5. Data Sources & Limitations

| Source | Type | Date |
|--------|------|------|
| ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 | Clinical trial registry | July 21, 2026 |
| OpenStreetMap (Nominatim + Overpass) | Provider/amenity geodata | July 21, 2026 |
| OSM nearby-place search | Local provider inventory | July 21, 2026 |

**Limitations:**
- OSM data may miss small or unlisted CPO providers (freelance practitioners, mobile clinics).
- Distance estimates to nearest CPO are approximations based on known regional providers.
- ClinicalTrials.gov counts may include multi-country studies counted per country, inflating US/European totals.
- Medicaid expansion status is current as of 2026 and may change.
