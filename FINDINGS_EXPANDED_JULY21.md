### 2.3 Trial Spotlights

**Access Socket (NCT07592325):** First randomized crossover trial comparing a flexible hybrid socket (ProtheFLEX zones) vs. conventional rigid socket for transtibial amputees. 25 participants, 7 French centers. Primary outcome: Patient Specific Functional Scale (PSFS). Sponsored by French health insurance. This design could enable more comfortable, adaptable fittings for rural patients who cannot travel to specialists.

**Bio Leg BAM (NCT07613502):** Comparing C-Leg (standard MPK) vs. Bio Leg (powered MPK) for transfemoral amputees. 12 participants at Brooks Rehabilitation, Jacksonville FL. Key outcomes: 6-Minute Walk Test, metabolic efficiency, ground reaction force symmetry. Funded by University of Florida.

## 5. Key Takeaways

1. **Trial recruitment is thin:** Only 17.4% of all prosthetic studies actively recruit.
2. **Phase 3 trials are critically scarce:** Only 30 across 644 studies (4.7%).
3. **Academic sponsors dominate (76.5%):** Commercial translation is limited.
4. **Zero trial sites in WV, KY, or MS Delta** despite highest U.S. amputation rates.
5. **Zero CPO providers within 30 km** in all target regions.
6. **Medicaid non-expansion compounds the crisis** in WV and MS.
7. **Emerging tech (3D printing, smart sockets, powered knees) could lower costs** for rural deployments.
