## Recently Identified Trials

### NCT07519746 — PROINGA: Satisfaction & Quality of Life Among Prosthetic Users in Gaza
- **NCT ID**: NCT07519746
- **Status**: COMPLETED
- **Sponsor**: Yeditepe University
- **Collaborator**: Al-Azhar University – Gaza
- **Start Date**: 2025-09-01
- **Completion**: 2026-03-25
- **Enrollment**: 128 participants
- **Study Type**: Observational (cross-sectional)
- **Population**: Adult lower-limb prosthetic users (≥18 years, using prosthesis ≥6 months) in Gaza Governorate
- **Key Outcomes**: Patient satisfaction (SAT-PRO, PEQ, TAPES), Quality of Life (EQ-5D-5L, WHOQOL-BREF, SWLS)
- **Context**: Gaza War (2023) caused rise in traumatic amputations; limited access to prosthetic services makes rehabilitation difficult
- **Significance**: Demonstrates how conflict zones create acute prosthetic care gaps requiring dedicated trial infrastructure

## Key Observations

1. **The U.S. leads global prosthetic research** with 680 studies — yet within its own borders, care deserts persist in rural regions.
2. **113 actively recruiting trials** represent opportunities for patients in underserved areas to participate, but geographic barriers may prevent access.
3. **24 terminated trials** suggest challenges in enrolling and retaining participants — likely exacerbated in rural/underserved populations.
4. **134 studies with unknown status** may represent abandoned trials that could have addressed access gaps.
5. **Quality-of-life and satisfaction** are emerging as key endpoints, moving beyond purely functional outcomes.
6. **Conflict-related prosthetic needs** (Gaza, Iraq) highlight the intersection of trauma and access disparities globally.

## Implications for Access Atlas

- The wealth of U.S.-based trials (680) contrasts starkly with the near-zero clinical infrastructure in rural WV and MS Delta.
- Active recruiting trials (113) should be geographically mapped to identify whether they cluster in urban academic centers, leaving rural populations behind.
- Low enrollment in some trials may be partly due to logistical barriers in underserved regions — telehealth and mobile trial units could help.
