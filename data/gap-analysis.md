# Prosthetic & Orthotic Care Gap Analysis — Underserved Regions

## Executive Summary

Three U.S. regions were analyzed for prosthetic and orthotic care provider availability using OpenStreetMap and neighborhood analysis:
1. Rural West Virginia (Beckley area)
2. Eastern Kentucky (Floyd County / Hindman–Martin corridor)
3. Mississippi Delta (Greenville, Washington County)

**The overarching finding: all three regions suffer from severe prosthetic care deserts, with rural West Virginia having the most critical gap.**

---

## Region 1: Rural West Virginia (Beckley, WV)

### Provider Landscape
- **Known providers within 30 km: 0**
- No hospitals, orthotic/prosthetic laboratories, or O&P practitioners detected
- Nearest likely providers: Raleigh General Hospital (Beckley — ~5 km, may have limited services), CAMC Beckley (unlikely, no data found)
- **Estimated drive time to nearest full-service O&P**: 45–90+ minutes to Charleston or Lexington

### Population & Need
- Raleigh County population: ~76,000
- Rural, mountainous terrain with dispersed settlement
- High rates of opioid use disorder → elevated amputation risk
- Aging demographics → higher prosthetic need
- Median household income well below national average

### Gap Severity: 🔴🔴🔴 CRITICAL
- No local prosthetic evaluation, fitting, or follow-up care
- Patients must travel 60–120 miles round-trip for basic appointments
- Weather (snow/ice) further limits mobility and access
- Suspended or terminated trips = missed fittings = abandoned prostheses

### Recommended Interventions
- Mobile prosthetic clinic / van service
- Tele-rehabilitation for socket checks and alignment adjustments
- Training local healthcare workers in basic prosthesis maintenance
- Partnership with WVU School of Medicine for rural outreach

## Region 2: Eastern Kentucky (Floyd County)

### Provider Landscape
- **Known providers within 30 km: Limited (score 9.0, but relative)**
- Some general healthcare present; specialized O&P likely absent
- Nearby possible providers: King's Daughters Medical Center (Ashland, KY — ~30 km), Scenic Hill Surgical
- **Estimated drive time to nearest specialized O&P lab**: 60–120 minutes

### Population & Need
- Floyd County population: ~36,000
- One of Appalachia's most economically distressed counties
- Compound poverty, disability, and chronic disease
- Limited public transportation (score: 0/10)
- Residents in remote hollows face even greater barriers than the town center

### Gap Severity: 🔴🟡 MODERATE-HIGH
- Some healthcare exists, but specialized prosthetic services are distant
- Transportation barrier is the primary blocker (0 walkability, 0 public transit)
- The Hindman–Martin corridor has groceries but no medical services

### Recommended Interventions
- Tele-prosthetics program for remote fit checks
- Ride-share / volunteer driver networks for clinic visits
- Regional O&P satellite clinic in Pikeville or Prestonsburg
- Nonprofit partnerships (e.g., Limbs International, Range of Motion Project)

--

## Region 3: Mississippi Delta (Greenville, MS)

### Provider Landscape
- **Known providers within 30 km: 1**
  - Southeast Rehabilitation Hospital (Lake Village, AR) — 23.6 km away, 10-bed rehab facility
- No dedicated prosthetic/orthotic labs, practitioners, or fitting centers found
- **Nearest major prosthetics center**: Likely Memphis, TN (>100 km) or Little Rock, AR (~150 km)

### Population & Need
- Washington County population: ~46,000
- Among the poorest counties in America (per-capita income historically <$15,000)
- Deep agricultural economy with high injury and amputation rates
- Majority-Black population with documented healthcare disparities
- Zero public transportation
- Only 5 grocery stores within 30 km

### Gap Severity: 🔴🔴🔴 CRITICAL
- Complete absence of prosthetic services within reasonable distance
- Structural poverty barriers compounded by geographic isolation
- Historical underinvestment in Delta healthcare infrastructure

### Recommended Interventions
- Federally Qualified Health Center (FQHC) expansion for O&P services
- Mobile O&P unit with prosthetic training for local providers
- Partnership with University of Mississippi Medical Center for tele-prosthetics
- Policy advocacy for Delta healthcare infrastructure investment
- Community health worker program for amputation prevention education

| **Gap Severity** | **CRITICAL** | **MODERATE-HIGH** | **CRITICAL** |

## Unified Recommendations

1. **Mobile O&P Units**: Deploy prosthetic vans serving all three regions on rotating schedules
2. **Tele-Prosthetics**: Remote socket assessment, gait analysis, and alignment consultation via video
3. **Community Training**: Local healthcare workers trained in basic prosthetic maintenance and emergency adjustments
4. **Transportation Solutions**: Volunteer driver networks, Medicaid non-emergency medical transport expansion
5. **Data Integration**: Map this data into a live provider directory that updates as new facilities open
6. **Policy Advocacy**: Use gap data to support HRSA funding requests for rural prosthetic care

---

*This analysis uses OpenStreetMap data which may undercount smaller private practices. Cross-reference with VA prosthetic centers (many WV veterans access VA services in Lexington, KY or Louisburg, NC) and commercial O&P directories for completeness.*
