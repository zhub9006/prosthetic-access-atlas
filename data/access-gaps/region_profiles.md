### Composite Finding

All three underserved regions exhibit **zero prosthetic/orthotic care providers** within a 30 km radius. The nearest CPO is 130–200 miles away, implying a 3–4+ hour drive for basic prosthetic services. This is not a marginal gap — it is a **complete absence** of local access. These regions all lag on Medicaid expansion, compounding the geographic barrier with financial access challenges.

The Mississippi Delta is particularly critical: it has the highest amputation rates in the U.S. (driven by diabetes, vascular disease, trauma) yet the worst care access of any region studied.
