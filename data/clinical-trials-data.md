# Clinical Trial Data Extract

## Latest Prosthetic Studies with Prosthetic Limb Interventions

### NCT02366702 — Passive vs Powered Prosthetic Devices
- **Status:** Unknown (last: ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING)
- **Sponsor:** Southern California Institute for Research and Education
- **Collaborators:** Vanderbilt University
- **Phase:** Observational (case-crossover)
- **Enrollment:** 3 (actual)
- **Location:** VA Long Beach Healthcare System, CA
- **Summary:** Kinematic, kinetic, and metabolic comparison of bilateral transfemoral ambulation with passive prostheses vs powered prosthetic devices (BiOM). May indicate benefits for clinical application of powered devices.
- **PI:** Michael Goldfarb, Ph.D.

---

### NCT02666859 — VR Prosthetic Training
- **Status:** COMPLETED
- **Sponsor:** University of South Florida
- **Collaborators:** U.S. Army MRDC; TATRC
- **Phase:** Early Phase 1 (randomized)
- **Enrollment:** 10 (actual)
- **Location:** University of South Florida, Tampa, FL
- **Summary:** CAREN-based virtual reality for upper limb prosthetic training. Funded by U.S. Army. Showed promise for enhancing prosthetic rehabilitation.
- **PI:** Stephanie L. Carey, Ph.D.

---

### NCT07652619 — Motor Imagery for Gait Training
- **Status:** COMPLETED
- **Sponsor:** Ariel University (Israel)
- **Phase:** Interventional
- **Enrollment:** Feasibility pilot
- **Location:** Ariel University
- **Summary:** Motor imagery prior to or alongside initial prosthetic gait training in acute transtibial amputees. Tests whether pre-training with mental imagery improves prosthetic gait outcomes.

---

### NCT06243549 — Lower Back Pain in Amputees with Prosthetics
- **Status:** ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING
- **Sponsor:** University of Bath (UK)
- **Collaborators:** University Hospitals Bristol; Portsmouth Hospitals NHS Trust
- **Phase:** Observational (prospective cohort)
- **Enrollment:** 30 (estimated)
- **Location:** UK (multiple NHS sites)
- **Summary:** 12-month study tracking biomechanical and psychological mechanisms of lower back pain in lower-limb amputees. Aims to inform patient-centered rehabilitation programs.
- **PI:** Alexandra Withey

---

### NCT02424903 — Prosthetic Joint Infection Cohort Study
- **Status:** UNKNOWN (not yet recruiting at start)
- **Sponsor:** Pro-Implant Foundation
- **Collaborators:** Charité University, Berlin
- **Phase:** Observational (prospective cohort)
- **Enrollment:** 5,000 (estimated)
- **Location:** Germany (multi-center)
- **Summary:** Largest prosthetic study identified. Compares treatment approaches for prosthetic joint infections in hip, knee, and shoulder revisions. Establishes biobank of pathogens.
- **PI:** Andrej Trampuz
