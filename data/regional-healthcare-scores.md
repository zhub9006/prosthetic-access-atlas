## Beckley, West Virginia (37.78°N, -81.19°W)

- **Livability:** ~3.2/10 — Rural Appalachia
- **Healthcare:** 15+ pharmacies, ~5 dentists, 1 dialysis center; **0 prosthetic/orthotic providers**
- **Public Transit:** Minimal
- **Education:** Community colleges only; no university within 30 km
- **Critical Gap:** ARH Homecare Store (Oceana, ~15 km) sells basic DME but cannot provide custom prosthetic fitting, residual limb shaping, or gait training

## Hazard, Kentucky (37.25°N, -83.19°W)

- **Livability:** ~2.8/10 — Remote Appalachian hollow
- **Healthcare:** 3 pharmacies, 1 clinic, 1 counseling center; **0 prosthetic/orthotic providers**
- **Public Transit:** None
- **Education:** 1 community college
- **Critical Gap:** Even basic pharmacy access is limited; mountainous terrain makes travel extremely difficult in winter

## Greenville, Mississippi (33.41°N, -91.06°W — Mississippi Delta)

- **Livability:** ~3.5/10 — Rural Delta, highest amputation rate in the U.S.
- **Healthcare:** 4+ pharmacies, 1 clinic, 1 LTACH (90 beds); **0 prosthetic/orthotic providers**
- **Public Transit:** Limited
- **Education:** 1 community college
- **Critical Gap:** Southeast Rehabilitation Hospital (Lake Village, AR) is a 10-bed long-term acute care facility — **not a CPO-capable facility** — representing the only rehab infrastructure within 75 km

## Interpretation

The three underserved regions score at or below 3.5/10. The most critical deficiency is the **complete absence of CPO/prosthetic-orthotic providers within 50 km**. This is the defining gap.

**Broken care chain:** Amputation → prosthetist → fitting → rehabilitation → follow-up. In all three regions, the chain breaks at step one. Pharmacies and primary care exist, but no specialty prosthetic infrastructure.

**Key finding:** All three states (WV, KY, MS) have **not expanded Medicaid**, compounding geographic barriers to prosthetic care access.
