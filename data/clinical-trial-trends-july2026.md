## Key Insights

1. **Phase 3 scarcity**: Only 30 pivotal efficacy trials (4.7%) exist for 644 studies — the evidence base for prosthetic device effectiveness is critically thin.

2. **Active recruitment is low**: 112 trials (17.4%) are recruiting, and only 12 are enrolling by invitation — limiting access for underserved populations.

3. **Academic dominance**: 76.5% of research is academic-funded. Industry involvement is modest (16.8%), suggesting limited commercial innovation pipeline for prosthetic devices.

4. **Unknown-status crisis**: 20.8% of studies have unknown status, which undermines data reliability for open-access use and suggests poor follow-up on completed studies.

5. **Observational studies dominate**: 52.5% are N/A (observational/device registries), important for real-world evidence but not for establishing efficacy.

6. **Geographic gap**: None of the 644 studies appear to target these three underserved regions (WV, KY, MS Delta) — the evidence gap maps directly to the access gap.
