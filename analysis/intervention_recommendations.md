# Intervention Recommendations — Prosthetic Care Access Gaps

## Tier 1: Immediate (0–12 Months)

1. **Mobile Prosthetic Clinics** — Deploy van-based CPO units on rotating schedules through rural WV, KY counties and MS Delta parishes. Partner with existing hospital networks (CAMC, Baptist Health) for staging.
2. **Tele-Prosthetics Program** — Remote prosthetic fittings and follow-ups via video consultation. Pilot with WVU Extension and University of Kentucky Telehealth.
3. **Medicaid Policy Reform** — Advocate for WV and MS to honor CPO reimbursement and expand consenting provider networks.
4. **Emergency Transport Vouchers** — State-funded travel stipends ($50–$100/visit) for prosthetic appointments in underserved counties.

## Tier 2: Medium-Term (1–3 Years)

5. **CPO Relocation Incentives** — Tax credits and student loan forgiveness for CPOs who establish practices in rural counties.
6. **Community Health Worker (CHW) Training** — Train local residents in basic prosthetic maintenance, socket checks, and skin care to bridge between appointments.
7. **3D-Printed Prosthetics Pilot** — Partner with community college fab labs to produce low-cost prosthetic components locally.
8. **University CPO Residency Pipeline** — WVU, University of Kentucky, and University of Mississippi Medical Center create rural rotations in amputation care and rehabilitation.

## Tier 3: Long-Term (3–5 Years)

9. **Rural-Inclusive Clinical Trials** — Require prosthetics studies funded by FDA or NIH to include rural sites in WV, KY, and MS.
10. **Industry Access Commitments** — Major prosthetic manufacturers (Össur, Ottobock, Fillauer) commit to serving rural provider deserts.
11. **Permanent Prosthetic Hubs** — Establish 3 regional CPO hubs (Charleston WV, Lexington KY, Memphis TN) with satellite mobile units.
12. **Preventive Amputation Care** — Diabetes and vascular disease screening programs in the Amputation Belt to reduce amputation incidence.
