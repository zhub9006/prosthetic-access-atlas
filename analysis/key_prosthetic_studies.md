## Upper Limb Prosthetics — Cutting-Edge Device Trials

### NCT05990062 — KSH Variable-Compliance Socket System (RECRUITING)
- **Sponsor**: VA Office of Research and Development (FED)
- **Status**: RECRUITING (started June 2025, completion est. Sept. 2028)
- **Condition**: Transradial amputation
- **Innovation**: New textile-based (athletic shoe technology) prosthetic socket/harness design using advanced materials to improve fit and comfort vs. traditional hard carbon-fiber sockets
- **Phases**: 3 (design, validation, expanded testing of 25 Veterans)
- **Significance**: Addresses the #1 reason for prosthesis abandonment — socket discomfort/poor fit
- **Relevant to gap analysis**: VA-based trial — could be model for veteran rural access, but no rural enrollment sites

### NCT07325708 — Phantom X Implantable EMG Sensor System — CYBORG Trial (RECRUITING)
- **Sponsor**: Phantom Neuro Inc. (INDUSTRY)
- **Status**: RECRUITING (started April 2026, n=10)
- **Conditions**: Transradial amputation, wrist disarticulation
- **Innovation**: First-in-human implantable EMG sensor array for myoelectric prosthesis control — direct muscle signal recording without surface electrodes
- **Phase**: NA (early feasibility)
- **Significance**: Addresses signal fidelity problem of surface EMG; offers bidirectional control + sensory potential
- **Relevant to gap analysis**: Only facility at specific centers; not accessible from rural regions

### NCT07032753 — Neuromusculoskeletal Interface for Bionic Arms / e-OPRA (NOT YET RECRUITING)
- **Sponsor**: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (OTHER) + Integrum AB (INDUSTRY) + NINDS (NIH)
- **Status**: NOT YET RECRUITING (start est. Jan. 2026)
- **Conditions**: Transhumeral amputation, TMR surgery patients
- **Innovation**: Randomized crossover trial comparing osseointegrated (OPRA) vs. electronic osseointegrated (e-OPRA) implant systems — direct skeletal attachment eliminating socket, with implanted EMG + sensory feedback
- **Phase**: First-in-human (n=8)
- **Significance**: Removes socket entirely — revolutionary for comfort/function; but requires complex surgery
- **Relevant to gap analysis**: Only specialized academic center (Chicago); requires TMR surgery first

### NCT06886295 — Intuitive, Bidirectional Multi-articulated Prostheses (RECRUITING)
- **Sponsor**: Sant'Anna School, Bologna, Italy (OTHER)
- **Status**: RECRUITING (2025–2026)
- **Conditions**: Upper limb amputation
- **Innovation**: Non-invasive upper limb prosthesis integrating ML-based myoelectric control + vibrotactile sensory feedback in unsupervised home use
- **Design**: Randomized crossover (n=14), 4-week home trials
- **Note**: International site but representative of global parallel effort

## Lower Limb / Drop Foot / Pediatric Prosthetics

### NCT04275973 — Evaluating Mobility Interventions in the Real World (COMPLETED)
- **Sponsor**: University of Wisconsin-Madison (OTHER) + DoD (FED)
- **Status**: COMPLETED (results just reported Dec 2025!)
- **Conditions**: Lower limb amputation, drop foot, MS
- **Innovation**: Wearable sensor-based real-world biomechanical comparison of prostheses/orthoses — passive vs. hydraulic vs. microprocessor ankles; caAFO vs. FES for drop foot
- **Enrollment**: 20 actual participants
- **Significance**: Directly relevant to functional outcomes research; results just in

### NCT04069793 — Powered Multifunction Wrist (COMPLETED)
- **Sponsor**: Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (OTHER)
- **Status**: COMPLETED (results available)
- **Conditions**: Transradial amputation at hand/wrist
- **Enrollment**: 9 actual
- **Innovation**: Measured functional importance of 2-DOF powered wrist vs. single-DOF wrist — evidence for clinical decision-making

### NCT02921295 — College Park Sidekick Feet vs. Stubby Prosthesis (COMPLETED)
- **Sponsor**: University of Pittsburgh (OTHER) + College Park Industries (Industry)
- **Location**: Pittsburgh, PA
- **Enrollment**: 1 (crossover design)
- **Condition**: Bilateral transfemoral amputation
- **Innovation**: Articulated "Sidekick" stubbies vs. conventional rigid stubbies — mobility and balance comparison

### NCT03088566 — D-Foot Implementation at Prosthetics & Orthotics Dept (COMPLETED)
- **Sponsor**: Sahlgrenska University Hospital, Sweden (OTHER)
- **Condition**: Diabetic foot screening
- **Innovation**: Standardized web-based foot screening tool (D-Foot) for prosthetic/orthotic departments — applicable to diabetic amputee prevention programs

## Women Veterans / Health Equity

### NCT03733054 — Understanding Prosthetic Needs and Outcomes in Women Veterans (COMPLETED)
- **Sponsor**: VA Office of Research and Development (FED)
- **Status**: COMPLETED (n=324 actual)
- **Condition**: Lower extremity amputation
- **Innovation**: Largest study to date on women veterans with LEA — characterized gender disparities in prosthetic prescription, use, satisfaction, and functional mobility
- **Significance**: Critical equity data; women veterans rarely studied
- **Context**: VA cares for 1,200+ women with major LEA — outcomes nearly unstudied before this

## Industry Newcomer — New Device Platform

### NCT04043234 — RESCU System for Robust Prosthesis Control (COMPLETED)
- **Sponsor**: Infinite Biomedical Technologies (INDUSTRY)
- **Status**: COMPLETED (2024, results posted 2024–2025)
- **Conditions**: Upper limb amputation
- **Enrollment**: 4 (single-case experimental design)
- **Innovation**: RESCU control software — retrospectively supervised ML algorithm that adapts to user over time, improving pattern recognition without prospective recalibration
- **Significance**: Industry approach to AI-driven prosthesis control; low enrollment demonstrates feasibility study barriers

## Summary: Trial Geography Gaps

| Trial Category | Gap Implication |
|---------------|----------------|
| All major upper limb trials at urban academic/VA centers | No rural enrollment sites exist |
| VA KSH trial — 3 phases at specific VAs | Rural Veterans must travel to urban VA hubs |
| e-OPRA requires TMR + complex infrastructure | Only available at Shirley Ryan (Chicago) |
| Industry trials limited to 4–10 participants at specialized centers | Device innovation not diffusing to rural practice |
| Women Veterans trial (n=324) was nationwide administrative — no rural clinical sites | Even national studies miss rural populations |

*Source: ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 detailed study records, July 2026*
