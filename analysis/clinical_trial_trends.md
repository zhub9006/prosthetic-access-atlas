# Clinical Trial Trends — Detailed Analysis

## Data Source: ClinicalTrials.gov
**Query date:** 2026-07-19  
**Total prosthetic studies:** 644  
**Limb prosthesis-specific studies:** 360  

## Status Trends

### All Prosthetic Studies (644)
- **COMPLETED:** 271 (42.1%) — Majority of research is observational/device registries that have concluded
- **RECRUITING:** 112 (17.5%) — Active enrollment is modest; only ~1 in 6 studies is currently recruiting
- **UNKNOWN:** 134 (20.8%) — A significant portion have uncertain status, suggesting limited follow-up
- **TERMINATED/WITHDRAWN:** 34 (5.2%) — Combined, about 5% of studies were stopped early

### Limb Prosthesis & Rehabilitation (360)
- Higher proportion of unknown status (103/360 = 28.6%) reflecting the niche nature of prosthetic research
- Only 3 studies currently recruiting out of subset analyzed

## Phase Trends

The heavy skew toward N/A (observational/device) phases reflects:
1. Prosthetic research is largely device evaluation and registry-based
2. Fewer randomized controlled trials exist for prosthetic interventions
3. The field is still maturing toward formal interventional study designs

### Key Observation
Only **~13.6% of all prosthetic studies** are in formal interventional phases (Phase 1–3). This means the vast majority of prosthetic research is descriptive, supporting the need for more rigorous clinical trials.

## Sponsor Trends

- **Academic/Other dominates (76.5%):** Prosthetic research is primarily university- and institution-driven
- **Industry participation is relatively low (16.8%):** Suggests limited commercial interest or investment in prosthetic innovation
- **Federal/NIH funding is minimal (0.9% under Network+NIH, 5.8% Federal+Other):** There is a funding gap for prosthetic care access research

## Geographic Trends

- **US leads with ~680 prosthetic studies** and 327 limb-prosthesis studies
- **Limb prosthesis research is concentrated in wealthy nations** — US, France, Denmark, Germany, Italy
- **Low-income countries are underrepresented** despite having highest amputation rates (diabetes, conflict, trauma)
- **The MS Delta, WV, and KY regions have ZERO prosthetic clinical trials targeting local access gaps**

## Implications for Access Atlas

1. The lack of recruiting prosthetic studies in rural/underserved regions means patients there cannot participate in cutting-edge research
2. Most prosthetic research happens at academic medical centers in urban areas
3. There is a clear need for pragmatic trials that include rural and underserved populations
4. The 3-country study (Gaza, NCT07519746) is a model for context-specific prosthetic research in resource-limited settings
