# Access Gap Analysis — Prosthetic & Orthotic Care Providers

## Overview

Three US regions were identified as underserved for prosthetic and orthotic care based on amputation prevalence, poverty rates, and distance to the nearest Certified Prosthetist-Orthotist (CPO). This analysis maps healthcare provider presence within 30 km of regional hubs to quantify coverage gaps.

## Recommended Interventions

1. **Mobile CPO units** — Deploy traveling prosthetic/orthotic fitting centers serving all three regions on rotating schedules
2. **Telehealth-enabled prosthetic care** — Allow CPOs to consult and fit patients remotely, reducing travel burden
3. **Federal grant programs** for CPO training in Appalachia and the Delta
4. **Industry engagement** to develop affordable, rugged prosthetic devices suited to rural environments
5. **Medicaid expansion** in WV, KY, and MS — a single policy change could unlock coverage for thousands
6. **Community health worker programs** — train local personnel in basic prosthetic maintenance and fitting
