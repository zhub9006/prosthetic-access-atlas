**Critical Insight:** Only **4 Phase 3 trials** exist in the focused search. Phase 3 trials are the gold standard for prosthetic device efficacy and are needed for FDA approval. With 2 million US amputees and 185K new amputations/year, this evidence gap is dangerous.

## Sponsor Distribution (US Prosthetic Amputation Subset, n=69)

| Sponsor | Count | % | Impact |
|---------|-------|---|--------|
| OTHER (Academic/Non-Profit) | 35 | 50.7% | Limited commercial impact; grant-dependent |

| INDUSTRY | 13 | 18.8% | **Only source for commercial device translation** |
| NIH | 1 | 1.4% | **Critically low** — NIH funding for prosthetics is minimal |

## Key Findings
1. **Phase 3 trials are critically scarce (3-5% of all studies)**
2. **Academic sponsorship dominates (~50%)** — limits commercial innovation pipeline
3. **NIH funding for prosthetics is nearly non-existent (1.4%)**
4. **Industry sponsorship is minimal (~19%)** — no commercial market incentive

## Recommended Actions
1. **Mandate NIH funding parity** for prosthetic/orthotic research
2. **Require Phase 3 diversity** — trials must include underserved regions
3. **Incentivize industry** sponsor participation in rural areas
4. **Fast-track** prosthetics devices that address diabetic/amputation needs
