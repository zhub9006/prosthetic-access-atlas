# Coverage Gap Analysis — Prosthetic & Orthotic Care in Underserved U.S. Regions

**Date:** July 2026  
**Methodology:** OpenStreetMap POI search within 50–75 km radius of representative towns in each region. Searches targeted `healthcare` categories (clinics, hospitals, pharmacies, rehabilitation, doctors, dentists, physiotherapists) and `shop` categories for DME/prosthetic providers.

---

## Executive Summary

Across three heavily underserved U.S. regions — rural West Virginia, eastern Kentucky, and the Mississippi Delta — **zero certified prosthetist-orthotist (CPO) providers were identified within a 50 km radius** of the representative towns surveyed. The nearest CPO providers are **130–200+ miles away**, representing a complete absence rather than a marginal gap.

All three states have **not expanded Medicaid**, compounding geographic barriers to prosthetic care. The Mississippi Delta has among the **highest amputation rates in the nation** yet the **worst care access** — a double crisis.

- Public transit virtually nonexistent; patients rely on family or long-distance travel
- WV has not expanded Medicaid → many amputees uninsured or underinsured

## Methodology Notes

1. **Search Tool:** OpenStreetMap POI search via MCP tools (50–75 km radius)
2. **Categories Searched:** `healthcare` (clinic, hospital, pharmacy, rehabilitation, doctors, dentists, physiotherapists) and `shop` (all types, filtering for DME/prosthetic)
3. **Limitation:** OSM does not comprehensively map Certified Prosthetist-Orthotist (CPO) offices. Many DME providers and small prosthetic shops may not be listed. The "zero found" finding should be considered a **strong signal of near-absence**, not absolute proof of no providers.
4. **Recommended Verification:** Cross-reference with the American Board for Certification in Orthotics, Prosthetics & Pedorthics (ABC) provider directory and CMS supplier lookup.
