# Prosthetic & Orthotic Care Coverage Gap Analysis

*Geographic audit using OpenStreetMap data. Search radius: 50 km from each region center.*

---

## Executive Summary

**Finding: Zero prosthetic or orthotic care providers were identified within 50 km of any of the three target underserved regions.** This represents a severe access gap for populations with high rates of diabetes, vascular disease, and occupational injury — the leading causes of limb loss in the United States.

## Root Causes

1. **Workforce shortage**: Certified prosthetists/orthotists (CPOs) concentrate in urban areas; rural economics cannot support full-time practices
2. **Reimbursement gaps**: Medicaid reimbursement rates in WV, KY, and MS are among the lowest nationally for prosthetic services
3. **Infrastructure deficit**: No clinical trial sites, no teaching hospitals, no prosthetic residency programs in these regions
4. **Transportation barriers**: Many residents lack reliable vehicles; public transit is virtually nonexistent
5. **Comorbid complexity**: High rates of diabetes, vascular disease, and substance use disorders complicate prosthetic candidacy and follow-up care

## Recommendations

1. **Telehealth prosthetic consultations**: Expand remote fitting and follow-up capabilities
2. **Mobile prosthetic clinics**: Deploy periodic outreach vans from urban centers
3. **3D-printed prosthetic solutions**: Technologies like the LIMBER UniLeg (see clinical-trials-analysis.md) could enable on-demand fabrication
4. **Policy advocacy**: Push for improved Medicaid reimbursement for prosthetic services in underserved states
5. **Community health worker integration**: Train local CHWs to provide basic prosthetic maintenance and identify complications early
6. **Clinical trial site expansion**: Advocate for trial sites in rural areas to improve both access and evidence
