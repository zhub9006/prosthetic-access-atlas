## Selected Key Studies

### Lower-Limb Prosthesis Research

#### NCT05287646 — Dynamic Stereo X-Ray for Socket Fit Optimization
- **Status:** RECRUITING
- **Sponsor:** VA Office of Research and Development (FED)
- **Phase:** N/A (Observational/Device)
- **Enrollment:** 21 (ESTIMATED)
- **Start Date:** January 2024
- **Completion:** March 2027 (ESTIMATED)
- **Location:** Rutgers University, University of Rhode Island
- **Focus:** Quantifying 3D bone and skin movement in residual limb-socket interface using dynamic stereo x-ray. Comparing suction vs. elevated vacuum suspension systems.
- **Significance:** Addresses fundamental knowledge gap in prosthetic fit biomechanics — directly relevant to reducing pain, gait deviations, and skin breakdown.

#### NCT02366702 — Passive vs. Powered Prosthetic Devices (Bilateral Transfemoral)
- **Status:** UNKNOWN / LAST KNOWN ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING
- **Sponsor:** Southern California Institute for Research and Education
- **Collaborator:** Vanderbilt University
- **Phase:** N/A (Observational)
- **Enrollment:** 3 (ACTUAL)
- **Start Date:** February 2015
- **Completion:** February 2020 (ESTIMATED)
- **Location:** VA Long Beach Healthcare System
- **Focus:** Kinematic, kinetic, and metabolic comparison of bilateral transfemoral ambulation with passive vs. powered prosthetic devices.
- **Significance:** Pilot study on powered knee-ankle prostheses — early evidence for powered device benefits but small sample limits generalizability.

### Upper-Limb Prosthesis Research

#### NCT02848846 — NEBIAS: Neurocontrolled Bidirectional Artificial Upper Limb
- **Status:** UNKNOWN / LAST KNOWN RECRUITING
- **Sponsor:** Paolo Maria Rossini, Catholic University of the Sacred Heart (Italy)
- **Phase:** N/A (Interventional)
- **Enrollment:** 2 (ESTIMATED)
- **Start Date:** June 2016
- **Completion:** December 2018 (ESTIMATED)
- **Location:** Fondazione Policlinico Agostino Gemelli, Rome, Italy

### Prosthetic Joint Infection Research

## What Would Help Close the Gap

1. **Travel grants or mobile trials** — bring prosthetic research to underserved populations
2. **Multi-site pragmatic trials** — embed prosthetic outcomes research in community health centers
3. **Industry-academic partnerships** — leverage academic sites in underserved regions to attract industry trials
4. **Telehealth-enabled prosthetics** — remote fitting and monitoring can fill the CPO access gap
5. **Policy advocacy** — Medicaid expansion in WV, KY, and MS combined with prosthetics coverage mandates
