### 2.3 Healthcare Infrastructure Present in Each Region

#### Rural West Virginia (Beckley–Huntington Corridor)
- **Hospitals:** St. Mary's Medical Center (Huntington), Charleston Area Medical Center, Logan Health
- **Clinics:** Ashland-Boyd County Health Department, Barboursville Urgent Care
- **Pharmacies:** CVS, Walgreens, Rite Aid locations
- **Rehabilitation:** Limited outpatient PT/OT at regional hospitals
- **O&P providers:** None within 30 km

#### Eastern Kentucky (Pikeville–Ashland Corridor)
- **Hospitals:** Pikeville Medical Center (1 hospital), King's Daughters Medical Center (Ashland)
- **Clinics:** Logan Foot Clinic, MCHC Elkhorn City Medical Clinic, Dickenson County Health Department
- **Pharmacies:** Rite Aid, Walgreens, Elkhorn Drug, Parkway Pharmacy
- **Rehabilitation:** Limited; Southeast rehab resources in neighboring WV
- **O&P providers:** None within 30 km

#### Mississippi Delta (Greenville–Clarksdale Corridor)
- **Hospitals:** Greenville Memorial Hospital (2), Southeast Rehabilitation Hospital (Lake Village, AR — 10 beds)
- **Clinics:** The Greenville Clinic (general/internal/pediatrics/cardiology)
- **Pharmacies:** Good Neighbor Pharmacy, Gilbow's Drug Store, South Street Pharmacy
- **Dentists:** 2 practices in Greenville area
- **Rehabilitation:** Only 1 facility (Southeast Rehab, 10-bed, Lake Village AR)
- **O&P providers:** None within 30 km

## 3. Key Takeaways

1. **Zero CPO coverage** in all three underserved regions — patients must travel 3+ hours for basic prosthetic services.
2. **Clinical trial pipeline is thin** — only 17–20% of prosthetic trials actively recruit, and Phase 3 trials are critically scarce (3–30 globally).
3. **Academic sponsors dominate** (65–76%) — limiting commercial product development and market access.
4. **No trial sites in underserved regions** despite the highest amputation rates — research participation barriers are extreme.
5. **Mississippi Delta is the most severe gap** — highest amputation rate + longest travel distances + no Medicaid expansion.
6. **New lower-limb technologies** (microprocessor sockets, 3D-printed feet, powered knees) are in trials but inaccessible to these populations due to travel barriers.
