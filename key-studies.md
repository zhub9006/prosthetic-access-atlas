# Key Clinical Studies

## Detailed Breakdown of 9 Highlighted Prosthetic Trials

---

### Study 1: MIRA — Myoelectric Implantable Recording Array
- **NCT ID:** NCT05768802
- **Status:** 🟢 RECRUITING
- **Phase:** Unknown
- **Conditions:** Amputation; Amputation; Traumatic, Hand
- **Sponsor:** Boninger, Michael, MD
- **Country:** United States
- **Intervention:** Myoelectric Implantable Recording Array (MIRA) — implantable intramuscular device
- **Summary:** Evaluates how well a new type of myoelectric prosthesis works. MIRA is implanted under the skin (vs. surface electrodes) and senses muscle electrical signals for improved prosthesis control. Device stays implanted up to 1 year. After implantation, training occurs to learn control via residual limb muscles.
- **Significance:** May revolutionize myoelectric control by moving from external electrodes to implantable arrays.

---

### Study 2: Powered Ankle Prosthesis with Neural Recording
- **NCT ID:** NCT07645729
- **Status:** 🟢 RECRUITING
- **Phase:** Unknown
- **Conditions:** Amputation
- **Sponsor:** Massachusetts Institute of Technology
- **Country:** United States
- **Interventions:** Powered Prosthesis; Prescribed Prosthesis
- **Summary:** Evaluates a powered ankle prosthesis with bidirectional control via neural recording and cutaneous stimulation. Assesses sense of embodiment, gait symmetry, and stability during free-space and walking tasks.
- **Significance:** Potential to improve bionic ankle integration with the human body for daily life.

---

### Study 3: Weight-bearing Training for Trans-tibial Amputees
- **NCT ID:** NCT05095805
- **Status:** ✅ COMPLETED
- **Phase:** Unknown
- **Conditions:** Amputation
- **Sponsor:** University Rehabilitation Institute, Republic of Slovenia
- **Country:** Slovenia
- **Interventions:** Additional weight-bearing exercise (Nintendo Wii Balance Board) vs. Standard weight-bearing exercise
- **Summary:** Pilot RCT using off-the-shelf devices (Wii Balance Board) and COP analysis for weight-bearing training in trans-tibial amputees receiving their first prosthesis.
- **Significance:** Low-cost, accessible training approach that could be replicated in rural settings.

---

### Study 4: Transdermal Compress Device
- **NCT ID:** NCT06134167
- **Status:** 🟢 RECRUITING
- **Phase:** Unknown
- **Conditions:** Prosthesis and Implants
- **Sponsor:** Balmoral Medical Company
- **Country:** United States (9 sites)
- **Intervention:** Transdermal Compress Device
- **Summary:** Evaluates safety and effectiveness of the Transdermal Compress device for participants with transfemoral amputations. Prospective, multicenter, single-arm, open-label design.
- **Significance:** Multi-site U.S. study addressing socket comfort issues.

---

### Study 5: Moisture Management Liner
- **NCT ID:** NCT04427007
- **Status:** ✅ COMPLETED
- **Phase:** Unknown
- **Conditions:** Amputation Stumps, Amputees, Prosthesis User
- **Sponsor:** Liberating Technologies, Inc.
- **Country:** United States
- **Interventions:** Moisture Management Liner vs. Control Liner
- **Summary:** Tests a silicone liner approach that removes sweat and passively conducts heat using thermally conductive elastomers. Works with the Intrasocket Cooling Element (ICE) device.
- **Significance:** Addresses a major complaint of prosthesis users — heat and moisture buildup.

---

### Study 6: Cyberlegs++ Ortho-prosthesis
- **NCT ID:** NCT03376919
- **Status:** ❓ UNKNOWN
- **Phase:** Unknown
- **Conditions:** Lower Limb Amputation Above Knee (Injury)
- **Sponsor:** Vrije Universiteit Brussel
- **Country:** Belgium / Italy
- **Intervention:** CLs++ gait training
- **Summary:** Assesses efficacy and usability of the Cyberlegs++ active prosthesis for transfemoral amputees. International project with 16 patients (8 in Belgium, 8 in Italy).
- **Significance:** May be stalled; outcome data could inform future active prosthesis development.

---

### Study 7: ASSIST — Interim Socket System
- **NCT ID:** NCT05196100
- **Status:** 🟡 ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING
- **Phase:** Unknown
- **Conditions:** Amputation
- **Sponsor:** University of Hull
- **Country:** United Kingdom
- **Intervention:** Amparo confidence socket
- **Summary:** Explores acceptability of an interim socket system for new below-knee amputation patients and NHS healthcare providers.
- **Significance:** Could reduce time and cost of initial prosthetic fitting; relevant to NHS and similar systems.

---

### Study 8: ITAP — Intraosseous Transcutaneous Amputation Prosthesis
- **NCT ID:** NCT02491424
- **Status:** ✅ COMPLETED
- **Phase:** Unknown
- **Conditions:** Traumatic Amputation of Lower Extremity
- **Sponsor:** Stryker Orthopaedics
- **Country:** United Kingdom
- **Intervention:** Direct skeletal fixation of ITAP to lower limb amputees
- **Summary:** Studies direct skeletal fixation of prosthetic limbs following trans-femoral amputation using the ITAP™ system.
- **Significance:** Surgical prosthetic approach that eliminates socket fitting; completed study may provide long-term outcome data.

---

### Study 9: Characterizing Ankle Function for Prosthesis Development
- **NCT ID:** NCT01784003
- **Status:** ✅ COMPLETED
- **Phase:** Unknown
- **Conditions:** Traumatic Amputation of Lower Extremity
- **Sponsor:** VA Eastern Colorado Health Care System
- **Country:** United States
- **Intervention:** Powered ankle-foot prosthesis
- **Summary:** Characterizes biological ankle joint function during walking and running on slopes to develop control parameters for powered ankle-foot prostheses. Then tested in a powered prosthesis worn by below-the-knee amputees.
- **Significance:** VA-funded; directly relevant to veteran prosthetic care; slope-ascending/descending data critical for real-world mobility.
