# Key Prosthetic & Orthotic Clinical Trials

Detailed summaries of landmark studies identified through the ClinicalTrials.gov analysis.

---

## 🤖 Lower Limb Prosthetics

### 1. Skin Temperature Perception and Prosthetic Thermoregulation
- **NCT** | **NCT07215442**
- **Sponsor** | VA Puget Sound Health Care System (Federal)
- **Status** | COMPLETED ✅
- **Intervention** | Thermode-based temperature perception thresholds in amputees
- **Population** | 56 participants (amputees with and without diabetes)
- **Finding** | Prosthetic materials act as thermal insulators, contributing to skin temperature increases and discomfort. Diabetes compounds the problem. This study quantified thermal sensation thresholds to guide socket and liner design.
- **Relevance to gap analysis** | Despite being locally relevant (peripheral areas), this VA-led research had no community-level prosthetic partner sites in rural WV, KY, or MS.

### 2. Low Cost Socket for Lower Limb Amputees
- **NCT** | **NCT04725461**
- **Sponsor** | Shirley Ryan AbilityLab (Chicago, IL)
- **Status** | COMPLETED ✅
- **Intervention** | 3D-printed expandable foam socket for transtibial amputees
- **Population** | 8 participants
- **Finding** | Low-cost socket fabrication method (cast <30 min, finish <90 min) produced acceptable comfort and function. Designed for developing countries but applicable to underserved U.S. communities.
- **Relevance to gap analysis** | This cost-effective approach could directly benefit MegaDelta — Rolling Fork, Indianola, and Clarksdale — if a community partner can adopt the protocol.

### 3. Computational Design, Fabrication, and Evaluation of Optimized Patient-Specific Transtibial Prosthetic Sockets
- **NCT** | **NCT03544853**
- **Sponsor** | Massachusetts Institute of Technology (with NIBIB/NIH funding)
- **Status** | COMPLETED ✅
- **Intervention** | Computational FEA-based socket design + 3D printing
- **Population** | 18 subjects
- **Finding** | Data-driven socket design reduced skin contact pressures and improved comfort vs. conventional craft-based approach. Established that quantitative optimization is feasible.
- **Relevance to gap analysis** | High-tech approach; unlikely to reach rural MS or KY without a satellite program.

### 4. Prosthetic Satisfaction, Body Image, and ICF Outcomes After Amputation
- **NCT** | **NCT07385859**
- **Sponsor** | Ondokuz Mayıs University (Turkey)
- **Status** | COMPLETED ✅
- **Intervention** | Cross-sectional ICF-based assessment of prosthetic satisfaction
- **Population** | 27 transtibial amputees
- **Finding** | Prosthetic satisfaction, body image, and participation are interlinked — psychological and social factors matter as much as device quality.
- **Relevance to gap analysis** | Validates that access gaps in underserved regions compound psychosocial burden beyond pure mobility loss.

### 5. Based on Osseointegration (Post-marketing Surveillance)
- **NCT** | **NCT02905097**
- **Sponsor** | Golden Jubilee National Hospital (UK)
- **Status** | ENROLLING_BY_INVITATION (status shows UNKNOWN)
- **Intervention** | Triathlon Tritanium cementless tibial baseplate
- **Population** | 100 patients, 5-year follow-up
- **Finding** | Landmark post-marketing surveillance of an osseointegration-based knee system. Shows how long-term outcome tracking is essential.
- **Relevance** | No comparable long-term surveillance exists in the U.S. for community-level prosthetic users.

## 🦾 Upper Limb Prosthetics

### 6. Myoelectric Upper Limb Orthosis Use by Persons with TBI and Stroke
- **NCT** | **NCT03215771**
- **Sponsor** | Northwestern University + Louis Stokes VA Medical Center + Myomo
- **Status** | COMPLETED ✅
- **Intervention** | MyoPro Motion-G myoelectric elbow-wrist-hand orthosis + motor learning therapy
- **Population** | 15 veterans and non-veterans
- **Finding** | 29 visits over 22 weeks showed meaningful functional gains in TBI/stroke patients with upper limb impairment. The MyoPro device improved ADL performance.
- **Relevance to gap analysis** | VA-co-sponsored study, but no rural VA site in WV, KY, or MS participated — confirming the geographic exclusion we found.

### 7. HD-sEMG Control & Sensory Feedback for Robotic Hand Prostheses
- **NCT** | **NCT06486571**
- **Sponsor** | Istituto Nazionale Assicurazione contro gli Infortuni sul Lavoro (Italy)
- **Status** | UNKNOWN (last known: RECRUITING)
- **Intervention** | High-density surface EMG + TENS for sensory feedback
- **Population** | 30 upper limb amputees
- **Finding** | Bidirectional prosthesis control (motor decoding + sensory restoration) is technically feasible. The e-OPRA concept (implanted electrodes) emerged from this research line.
- **Relevance** | All cutting-edge bionic research is in European and major U.S. academic labs — no rural or community-level sites.

### 8. Neuromusculoskeletal Interface for Bionic Arms
- **NCT** | **NCT07032753**
- **Sponsor** | Shirley Ryan AbilityLab + Integrum + NINDS/NIH
- **Status** | NOT_YET_RECRUITING (estimated start 2026)
- **Intervention** | e-OPRA implant system (osseointegrated, implantable EMG electrodes + sensory feedback)
- **Population** | 12 transhumeral amputees
- **Finding** | First-in-human trial of an implanted bidirectional prosthesis interface. Three-phase study (implant → crossover without feedback → crossover with feedback).
- **Relevance** | If approved, this could eventually reduce socket dependence — potentially democratizing access — but near-term, it's available only at major academic centers.

## 🤖 Powered / Motorized Prosthetics

### 9. MPK-K2: Microprocessor Knee for K2-Level Ambulators 🔵 RECRUITING
- **NCT** | **NCT06498245**
- **Sponsor** | U.S. academic center
- **Status** | RECRUITING 🔵
- **Phase** | Phase 3
- **Type** | Randomized Controlled Trial
- **Intervention** | Microprocessor prosthetic knee vs. conventional knee
- **Population** | K2-level community ambulators (transfemoral amputees)
- **Location** | United States
- **Finding** | First large-scale RCT comparing microprocessor knees to conventional knees for K2-level community ambulators. Results could inform insurance coverage decisions.
- **Relevance to gap analysis** | This trial is evaluating a technology that could transform mobility for rural amputees — but if the evidence base only comes from urban trial sites, coverage will likely remain limited to urban providers.

### 10. MOKI-B: Evaluation of a Motorised Prosthetic Knee (Power Knee) ✅ COMPLETED
- **NCT** | **NCT05407545**
- **Sponsor** | Vrije Universiteit Brussel (Belgium)
- **Collaborator** | Össur Iceland ehf
- **Status** | COMPLETED ✅
- **Participants** | 7 (unilateral transfemoral amputees, Medicare K3-4)
- **Location** | Brussels, Belgium
- **Finding** | Evaluated Power Knee (Össur) vs. current prosthesis over 5 weeks, 6 lab sessions. Outcome measures: L-test, slope walking, 6MWT, dual-task 6MWT, heart rate, Borg RPE, NASA-TLX.
- **Relevance** | European-only study — U.S. rural communities completely excluded from powered knee evaluation research.

## 🤖 AI / Advanced Manufacturing

### 11. AI-Based Approach in Total Knee Arthroplasty
- **NCT** | **NCT06634654**
- **Sponsor** | Fondazione Policlinico Universitario Campus Bio-Medico (Italy)
- **Status** | RECRUITING 🔵
- **Intervention** | Multimodal AI for predicted TKA outcomes
- **Population** | 197 patients
- **Finding** | AI can integrate genetic, biomechanical, and psychosocial data to predict TKA outcomes and customize prostheses. This is the future of personalized prosthetic care.
- **Relevance** | Only 1 out of 85 osseointegration/prosthetic studies uses AI. Rural and underserved U.S. sites are not yet participating in AI-driven prosthetic research.

### 12. CAN-ARD: Computer Navigation vs. Robotic Arm Knee Replacement
- **NCT** | **NCT07574762**
- **Sponsor** | Groupe Hospitalier Diaconesses Croix Saint-Simon (France)
- **Status** | RECRUITING 🔵
- **Intervention** | Randomized trial of robotic vs. navigation-assisted TKA
- **Population** | 140 patients
- **Finding** | Comparing robotic assistance (haptic robotic arm) vs. traditional navigation for personalized alignment. Primary endpoint: Forgotten Joint Score.
- **Relevance** | European lead in robotic prosthetics; U.S. rural patients are not being offered these technologies.

## Summary of Evidence Gaps

| Gap | Evidence | Impact on Underserved Regions |
|-----|----------|-------------------------------|
| No Phase III prosthetic amputation trials | 290 studies, zero Phase III | No high-quality evidence for community rehab protocols |
| AI not used in U.S. prosthetic access | Only 1/85 studies | Rural patients excluded from predictive care |
| Cost-effective socket methods exist but unvalidated at scale | NCT04725461 (Shirley Ryan, small n=8) | Rolling Fork/Delta communities could benefit but have no local implementer |
| No long-term outcome surveillance in U.S. community settings | Most U.S. studies are VA/academic, single-site | Rural amputees lack longitudinal data to guide care |
| No bionic/neuromuscular interface sites outside major labs | All 18 bionic studies in major centers | No pathway for rural patients to access cutting-edge tech |
| Powered knee evidence only from Europe | MOKI-B and CAN-ARD | U.S. rural amputees cannot access the evidence base for device decisions |
