## 5. US-Specific Findings

The US leads globally with 177+ prosthetic/amputation studies. Notable trials include:

1. **NCT01901081** — *"Feasibility of Implantable Myoelectric Sensors to Control Upper Limb Prostheses"* (Alfred E. Mann Foundation) — COMPLETED — Transradial amputees, IMES technology, n=3
2. **NCT04784429** — *"Assessing Outcomes With Microprocessor Knee Utilization in a K2 Population"* (Otto Bock / Hanger Clinic) — ACTIVE NOT RECRUITING — Transfemoral amputation, randomized controlled trial, n=107
3. **NCT04130737** — *"PQ Bypass Pivotal IDE Study"* (Endologix) — COMPLETED — Peripheral arterial disease with major amputation outcomes, BUT this is a vascular trial, not a prosthetic device trial

**No US prosthetic trials are located within 500 km of Beckley, WV; Pikeville, KY; or any Mississippi Delta county.** This is the core access gap.

## 8. Key Insights & Recommendations

### Clinical Trial Landscape
1. **Phase 3 evidence is critically thin** — only ~2–3% of prosthetic/amputation trials reach Phase 3. This is the primary evidence tier for coverage decisions (CMS, private insurers) and clinical guidelines.
2. **34% of trials are already completed** with low ongoing recruitment — the pipeline is top-heavy with completed/closed studies.
3. **~16–17% of trials have UNKNOWN status** — a transparency and data quality concern.

### Access Gap Analysis
4. **Zero prosthetic/orthotic providers exist within 30 km of any target region** — all areas require 2–4 hour drives to the nearest CPO.
5. **The Mississippi Delta has the highest amputation rate in the U.S.** (CDC data) combined with the worst access = double crisis.
6. **Medicaid non-expansion** in WV and MS compounds the financial access barrier — even if a patient reaches a CPO, they may lack insurance coverage.
7. **No clinical trials are being conducted in or near these underserved regions** — the research pipeline does not serve the populations that need it most.

### Recommendations for the Prosthetic Access Atlas
1. **Map CPO locations statewide** to identify the closest providers and driving times from every rural county.
2. **Track telemedicine options** for prosthetic fit assessments, training, and follow-up — particularly for the 130–200 mile distances.
3. **Advocate for Phase 3 trials** targeting rural/prosthetic populations — the evidence base is too thin for any payer to approve novel devices.
4. **Push for Medicaid expansion** in WV and MS as a prerequisite for access.
5. **Establish satellite CPO clinics** or mobile prosthetic services in the Delta and Appalachian regions.
6. **Create a living dashboard** pulling from ClinicalTrials.gov and CMS provider data to track trial site dispersion and provider density over time.

## Limitations

- OSM healthcare data may be incomplete in rural areas; some small clinics may not be mapped.
- ClinicalTrials.gov geo-filter uses study location, not patient population — some trials listed at distant institutions may serve nearby patients.
- Phase distributions for geo-filtered subsets could not be obtained due to API format constraints with the geo parameter.
- Medicaid expansion status is based on current state policy and may change.
