## Recruiting / Not Yet Recruiting Trials (Most Relevant)

### NCT07204912 — MIT Neural-Controlled Powered Prosthesis
- **Status:** RECRUITING
- **Sponsor:** Massachusetts Institute of Technology
- **Phase:** N/A (device study)
- **Enrollment:** 10 (estimated)
- **Conditions:** Transfemoral Amputation
- **Location:** MIT Media Lab, Cambridge, MA
- **PI:** Hugh Herr, MIT Media Lab
- **Key Innovation:** Neural-controlled powered knee-ankle prosthesis using surface EMG from residual limb
- **Outcomes:** Kinematic/kinetic biomechanics, gait symmetry, EMG control accuracy
- **Start Date:** May 2025
- **Estimated Completion:** May 2028
- **Relevance:** Most advanced prosthetic innovation identified. Single-site academic trial; rural access barrier is insurmountable for most target populations.

### NCT06844305 — Northwestern OPORP (Personalized Prosthetic Foot Prescription)
- **Status:** NOT_YET_RECRUITING
- **Sponsor:** Northwestern University (DoD-funded)
- **Phase:** N/A
- **Enrollment:** 50 Veterans (estimated)
- **Conditions:** Amputation of Lower Limb
- **Location:** Northwestern University Prosthetics-Orthotics Center, Chicago, IL
- **PI:** Matthew Major, PhD
- **Key Innovation:** Patients experience three VSPA Foot stiffness conditions to select preferred foot, then randomized to standard care vs. 8-week personalized PT
- **Outcomes:** Trinity Amputation Satisfaction Scale, PLUS-M, ABC Scale, TUG, 4-Square Step Test
- **Start Date:** Dec 2026 (estimated)
- **Relevance:** Veteran-focused personalization study. Chicago location excludes rural populations. Telerehab arm could be adapted for rural delivery.

## Recently Completed Trials (Completed Within Last 2 Years)

### NCT05569967 — Telerehabilitation vs Face-to-Face Prosthetic Rehab
- **Status:** COMPLETED
- **Sponsor:** Ankara Yildirim Beyazit University
- **Phase:** N/A
- **Enrollment:** 28 (actual)
- **Conditions:** Amputation
- **Location:** Ankara, Turkey
- **Key Innovation:** 8-week spinal stabilization training via telerehabilitation vs. face-to-face; outcomes non-inferior
- **Outcomes:** Gait speed, dynamic balance (4-Square Step Test), core muscle strength, joint position sense, PLUS-M
- **Start Date:** Oct 2022; Completion: Mar 2024
- **Relevance:** Most directly applicable trial for rural populations. Proves telerehabilitation for prosthetic rehab works. Could be deployed via telehealth to WV, KY, and MS Delta.

### NCT06821412 — Wireless EMG Control (ASTERISK System)
- **Status:** COMPLETED
- **Sponsor:** Liberating Technologies, Inc. (U.S. Army-funded)
- **Phase:** N/A
- **Enrollment:** 4 (actual)
- **Conditions:** Prostheses, Upper Limb Amputation Below Elbow
- **Location:** Liberating Technologies, Holliston, MA
- **PI:** Benjamin McDonald
- **Key Innovation:** Wireless non-invasive EMG electrodes (ASTERISK system) for myoelectric prosthesis control; eliminates wired connections
- **Outcomes:** OPUS-SD satisfaction (wireless: 2.89 vs wired: 3.11 — near parity)
- **Start Date:** Jun 2024; Completion: Jun 2025
- **Relevance:** Wireless design removes socket-fitting barriers. Small pilot but validated concept. Potential for rural deployment via telehealth fitting.
