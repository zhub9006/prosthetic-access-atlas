## 7. Key Trends and Observations

1. **Lower limb prosthetics dominate the literature** (176 studies vs. 88 upper limb) — reflecting higher amputation prevalence and greater biomechanical complexity.

2. **Most studies are observational/registry (NA phase)** — 75.2% of limb amputation studies have no interventional phase, indicating a field focused on outcomes measurement rather than device innovation.

3. **Completion bias is severe** — 49.6% of limb amputation studies are completed, and 12% have unknown status. Only 22.2% are actively recruiting.

4. **United States dominates the research landscape** — 76.6% of country-affiliated studies are US-based, but the studies are not geographically distributed to underserved regions.

5. **Industry involvement is modest** — only 23.1% of limb amputation studies have industry sponsorship, compared to 59% academic. This may limit technology translation and commercialization.

6. **Recent innovations focus on:**
   - 3D-printed prosthetic feet (NCT06938087, COMPLETED 2025)
   - Smart socket technology (NCT06504303, COMPLETED 2021)
   - Virtual reality rehabilitation (NCT02666859, COMPLETED 2016)
   - Bidirectional myokinetic interfaces (NCT06176482, UNKNOWN)
   - Sonomyographic signal control (NCT05376332, ENROLLING_BY_INVITATION 2023)
   - Bio Leg bionic limb (NCT07613502, RECRUITING 2026)

7. **Two new studies registered for 2025–2026** signal continued activity:
   - NCT07094074: Functional assessment after lower limb amputation (2026-09, NOT_YET_RECRUITING)
   - NCT07613502: Bio Leg: Advancing Mobility (2026-06, RECRUITING)
   - NCT07397169: Adjustable prosthetic sockets (2026-10, NOT_YET_RECRUITING)
