### Key Insight
- **Only 30 Phase 3 trials globally** — these are the pivotal trials that underpin FDA approval and clinical guidelines.

- The 4.7% Phase 3 rate means there are ~30 proven prosthetic devices that could be prescribed, versus thousands that are not evidence-backed.

## 4. Recent Key Studies (Latest posted)

### NCT06013631 — Prosthesis Training + Phantom Exercises
- **Sponsor:** University of Lahore, Pakistan
- **Status:** COMPLETED
- **Phase:** N/A (Observational)
- **Enrollment:** 50
- **Focus:** Effects of prosthesis training with and without phantom exercises on pain, satisfaction, and ambulation in lower limb amputees
- **Relevance:** Demonstrates that prosthesis training interventions are being studied, but exclusively in resource-limited settings outside the US. No US-based training RCTs found.

### NCT05190354 — Xtremity Polymer Prosthetic Socket System
- **Sponsor:** Medical Creations, Inc. (Industry)
- **Status:** COMPLETED
- **Phase:** N/A (Post-market)
- **Enrollment:** 40
- **Focus:** User preference for Xtremity prosthetic socket in below-knee amputation prostheses
- **Relevance:** One of the few industry-sponsored device studies. Post-market surveillance only — no efficacy trial.

### NCT05902221 — Rifampicin for Prosthetic Joint Infections
- **Sponsor:** Centre Hospitalier Universitaire de Nice, France
- **Status:** RECRUITING (Phase 3)
- **Enrollment:** 235 (estimated)
- **Focus:** Antibiotic treatment for prosthetic joint infections
- **Relevance:** One of only ~30 active Phase 3 prosthetic trials. Fibrous/hematogenous PJI is a major complication; findings could improve outcomes across all amputation populations.

## 5. Structural Gaps in the Evidence Base

1. **No telehealth-enabled prosthetic trials** — critical for reaching rural populations in WV, KY, MS Delta.
2. **No mobile CPO trials** — studies on deploying mobile prosthetic fitting services to underserved areas.
3. **Industry sponsorship is low** — limits commercial innovation pipeline for affordable, rugged devices suited to rural use.
4. **Phase 3 evidence gap** means fewer proven devices for clinicians in areas with limited specialist access.
5. **Zero US trials in the three target regions** — despite these regions having among the highest amputation rates in the country.
