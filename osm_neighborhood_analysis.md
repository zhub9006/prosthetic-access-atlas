## Key Insight

The neighborhood scores reveal that prosthetic care gaps are not simply about lack of healthcare infrastructure — they reflect a broader pattern of community deprivation. The two regions with the highest amputation rates (MS Delta) have the worst overall livability scores, while the region with the highest disability rate (WV) has decent surface-level amenities but zero specialized prosthetic capacity. In all four regions, the absence of social services, vocational rehabilitation, and public transit compounds the prosthetic access gap.
