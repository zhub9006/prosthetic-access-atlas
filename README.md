## Access Gap Summary (Detailed)

| Region | CPO 30km | Nearest CPO | Drive Mi | Drive Hrs | Medicaid | Amp Rate | Hospitals |
|--------|----------|-------------|----------|-----------|----------|----------|-----------|
| WV (Beckley) | 0 | Charleston, WV | ~190 | 3+ | No expanded | High | 3 |
| KY (Pikeville) | 0 | Lexington, KY | ~130 | 3+ | Limited | High | 1 |
| MS Delta (Greenville) | 0 | Memphis, TN | ~200 | 3+ | No expanded | Highest US | 2 |

### Key Gap Findings
- **Zero prosthetic/orthotic (CPO) providers** exist within 30 km of any of the three target regions
- The nearest CPO is **130–200+ miles away**, requiring 3+ hours of driving
- All three regions are served only by general/acute-care hospitals with no O&P specialty
- **WV and MS have not expanded Medicaid**; KY has limited expansion — further limiting coverage for prosthetic devices and fittings
- The Mississippi Delta has the **highest amputation rate in the United States** (driven by diabetes prevalence)

### Key Trial Insights
1. **Only 22.2% of limb-amputation prosthetic trials actively recruit** — the pipeline for new evidence is thin.
2. **Phase 3 efficacy trials are critically scarce** — only 4 in the broad prosthetic dataset, ~30 globally.
3. **75.2% of limb amputation studies are observational/registries** — limited interventional device research.
4. **Academic sponsors dominate (59–77%)** — limits commercial innovation and market translation.
5. **Zero prosthetic clinical trial sites** in WV, KY, or MS, despite high amputation rates in these regions.
