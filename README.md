## Key Insights

1. **Only 69 Phase 3 trials exist globally** — the evidence base for prosthetic device efficacy is critically thin.
2. **19.9% of studies have UNKNOWN status** — potential data quality and transparency concern.
3. **Academic-sponsored dominance (76.8%)** — limits commercial innovation pipeline and market translation.
4. **US leads globally** with 2,384 studies — yet has **zero trial sites** in WV, KY, or MS.
5. **380 trials actively recruiting** — but none in the regions with the highest amputation rates.
6. **MS Delta**: Highest amputation rate in the U.S. + zero healthcare detected in Clarksdale + worst prosthetic access + no Medicaid expansion = **double crisis**.

## Access Gap Summary — Regional Details

### Rural West Virginia (Beckley)
- CPO within 30 km: 0 | Nearest: Charleston, WV (59 mi, 1.3 hr)
- Healthcare (30 km): 7+ facilities | Medicaid: Not expanded
- Key barrier: WV did not expand Medicaid; mountainous terrain; coal economy decline

### Eastern Kentucky (Pikeville)
- CPO within 30 km: 0 | Nearest: Lexington, KY (140 mi, 2.7 hr)
- Healthcare (30 km): 11+ facilities | Medicaid: Limited expansion
- Key barrier: Appalachian isolation; no public transit

### Mississippi Delta (Clarksdale)
- CPO within 30 km: 0 | Nearest: Memphis, TN (75 mi direct, ~200 mi via local roads)
- Healthcare (30 km): **ZERO** | Medicaid: Not expanded
- Key barrier: Highest amputation rate + worst prosthetic access + no transit

### Additional Delta Points (Greenville, Indianola, Leland, Rolling Fork)
- All show the same zero-CPO pattern
- Memphis is the nearest hub at 57–67 mi direct
- Eastern Delta counties face 150–200+ mi drives
