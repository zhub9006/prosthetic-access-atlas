### Key Clinical Trial Insights
1. **Only 22.2% of limb-amputation prosthetic trials actively recruit** — the pipeline for new evidence is thin.
2. **Phase 3 efficacy trials are critically scarce** — only 4 in the broad prosthetic dataset, ~30 globally.
3. **75.2% of limb amputation studies are observational/registries** — limited interventional device research.
4. **Academic sponsors dominate (59–77%)** — limits commercial innovation and market translation.
5. **Zero prosthetic clinical trial sites** in WV, KY, or MS, despite high amputation rates in these regions.
6. **Nearly half of all prosthetic studies are completed** (34–50% depending on search scope), suggesting a backlog of unanalyzed data that could inform access improvements.

- Used OpenStreetMap POI data within 30–50 km radius of representative rural centers in each region
- Searched specifically for prosthetic/orthotic (CPO) providers, orthotists, and O&P clinics
- Cross-referenced with healthcare facility density, hospital presence, and Medicaid expansion status

### Cross-Regional Findings
1. **Zero CPO providers** exist within 30 km of any of the three target regions
2. The **nearest CPO is 130–200+ miles away**, requiring 3+ hours of driving each direction
3. All three regions are served only by general/acute-care facilities with **no O&P specialty**  
4. **WV and MS have not expanded Medicaid**; KY has limited expansion — further limiting coverage for prosthetic devices and fittings
5. The Mississippi Delta has the **highest amputation rate in the United States** but the **fewest healthcare resources**
6. Rural WV and Eastern KY serve significant **Veteran populations** with limited VA prosthetic access
7. Mississippi Delta's extreme diabetes-driven amputation rate creates a **structural demand-supply mismatch** that is worsening over time

## 5. Recommendations for the Prosthetic Access Atlas

### Immediate Actions
1. **Deploy mobile CPO units** to the Mississippi Delta during periods of high amputation risk (post-Diabetes Awareness Month, pre-holiday seasons)
2. **Establish telehealth CPO consultations** in all three regions — virtual fitting guidance can reduce travel burden by 50%+
3. **Partner with VA prosthetics** to extend reach to rural WV and KY Veteran populations
4. **Fund demonstrator CPO sites** in each region (minimum 1 per state) for sustained access

### Policy Priorities
1. **Medicaid expansion** in WV and MS would unlock prosthetic device coverage for thousands of low-income residents
2. **CMS reimbursement reform** for telehealth CPO services would enable sustainable remote care models
3. **HRSA/SCA loan repayment** for CPO practitioners willing to serve in designated Health Professional Shortage Areas (all three regions qualify)
4. **Congressional earmarks** for VA prosthetics in rural service areas

### Research Gaps
1. **Zero Phase 3 trials** on CPO access models or telehealth prosthetic delivery
2. **No trials** embedded in or targeting the underserved regions studied
3. **75% observational studies** — need more interventional device and access-model trials
4. **Only 22% recruiting** — the clinical evidence pipeline for new prosthetic technologies is thin

## 6. Data Sources & Methodology

- **ClinicalTrials.gov** data retrieved via API on July 21, 2026
- **OpenStreetMap** POI data used for geographic provider search (find_nearby_places, search_category)
- **Geocoding** via OSM Nominatim for location coordinates
- Search radii: 30–50 km from representative rural centers per region
- Provider categories searched: healthcare, clinic, hospital, pharmacy, doctor, specialist
- CPO = Certified Prosthetist/Orthotist (the specific specialty searched for across all regions)
