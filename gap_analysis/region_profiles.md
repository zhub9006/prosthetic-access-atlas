# Underserved Region Profiles — Prosthetic & Orthotic Care Gaps

## Methodology

For each region, we:
1. Geocoded a representative city in the underserved area
2. Searched OpenStreetMap within a 100km radius for healthcare providers
3. Filtered for prosthetics, orthotics, and rehabilitation categories
4. Cross-referenced against general healthcare infrastructure

## Summary: National Coverage Gaps

These three regions are not isolated cases. Pattern analysis reveals:

1. **Rural Appalachia** (WV, KY, VA, TN) — Persistent provider deserts, high amputation rates from vascular disease
2. **Mississippi Delta** — Deep poverty, high diabetes-driven amputation rates, zero prosthetics access
3. **Common barriers**: No O&P offices, no fabrication labs, no certified orthotists/prosthetists, transportation distances exceeding 100+ miles

### Recommended Next Steps
- Deploy mobile prosthetics clinics in these corridors
- Telehealth-enabled prosthetic fitting and alignment consultations
- Expand VA and Medicaid reimbursement for telehealth O&P services
- Establish O&P training pipelines in Appalachian and Delta communities
- Partner with 3D printing/scanning organizations for low-cost prosthetic fabrication in these regions
