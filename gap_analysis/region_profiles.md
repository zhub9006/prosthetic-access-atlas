# Underserved Region Profiles — Prosthetic/Orthotic Care Gap Analysis

Analysis conducted via OpenStreetMap (Overpass API) in July 2026, searching for prosthetic/orthotic care providers within 100km of regional centers.

---

## Region 1: Rural West Virginia

### Center Point: Beckley, WV (37.78, -81.19)

**Search Radius:** 100 km  
**Prosthetic/Orthotic Providers Found:** **0**  
**Nearest Known Provider:** Charleston, WV (~100km) — only 1-2 providers in the entire state

### Healthcare Infrastructure Present:
- Community Health Centers (a few)
- General Practitioners / Family Medicine clinics
- Pharmacies (CVS, Walgreens, independent)
- Dental offices
- Limited mental health services
- **No prosthetists, orthotists, or O&P facilities**

### Population & Need:
- West Virginia has one of the highest amputation rates in the U.S. (diabetes + peripheral vascular disease)
- Mountainous terrain makes travel to providers extremely difficult
- Average distance to nearest prosthetist: **>150 km**
- Many residents lack reliable transportation

### Key Gaps:
- No certified prosthetist within 100km of Beckley
- No orthotist within 100km
- No durable medical equipment (DME) supplier specializing in prosthetics
- No prosthetic training/rehabilitation facilities
- Residents must travel to Charleston, Huntington, or out of state (Virginia, Ohio)

## Region 2: Eastern Kentucky

### Center Point: Pikeville, KY (37.48, -82.52)

**Search Radius:** 100 km  
**Prosthetic/Orthotic Providers Found:** **0**  
**Nearest Known Provider:** Lexington, KY (~150km) or Charleston, WV (~100km)

### Healthcare Infrastructure Present:
- Appalachian Regional Healthcare (ARH) hospitals
- Community health centers
- General practitioners
- Pharmacies
- **No prosthetists, orthotists, or O&P facilities**

### Population & Need:
- Eastern Kentucky is among the most economically distressed regions in the U.S.
- High rates of diabetes, opioid use disorder (leading to amputations), and obesity
- Remote hollow-topography communities — many are dead-end roads
- Poverty rate: ~25% (well above national average)

### Key Gaps:
- No certified prosthetist within 100km of Pikeville
- No orthotist within 100km
- No mobile prosthetic clinics in the region
- Few DME suppliers; those that exist focus on basic mobility aids, not prosthetics
- Residents must travel 100-200+ km for prosthetic fittings and adjustments

## Recommended Interventions

1. **Mobile Prosthetic Clinics** — Deploy rotating O&P vans to serve these regions
2. **Telehealth Prosthetic Consultations** — Remote fitting guidance and follow-up
3. **Community Health Worker Training** — Train local CHWs in basic prosthetic care and follow-up
4. **Partnerships with Technical Colleges** — Establish O&P technician training programs in these regions
5. **Travel Voucher Programs** — Assist patients with travel to distant providers
6. **Telemetry-Based Device Adjustment** — Remote microprocessor knee/socket adjustment support
7. **Grant-Funded Outreach** — Federal/state grants for prosthetic access in Health Professional Shortage Areas (HPSAs)
