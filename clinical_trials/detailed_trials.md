## Trial 1: PROINGA (NCT07519746)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Title** | Satisfaction and Quality of Life Among Prosthetic Users in Gaza Governorate |
| **Acronym** | PROINGA |
| **Status** | COMPLETED |
| **Sponsor** | Yeditepe University (OTHER) |
| **Collaborators** | Al-Azhar University - Gaza |
| **Start Date** | 2025-09-01 (ACTUAL) |
| **Completion** | 2026-03-25 (ACTUAL) |
| **Enrollment** | 128 (ACTUAL) |
| **Type** | OBSERVATIONAL — Cross-sectional prevalence study |
| **Patient Registry** | Yes |
| **Condition** | Amputation, Prosthesis User, Satisfaction, Quality of Life |
| **Location** | Gaza hospitals, Gaza, Palestinian Territories |

**Brief Summary:**
In Gaza, the 2023 Gaza War caused a rise in traumatic amputations and limited access to healthcare and prosthetic services. This cross-sectional study evaluates adult lower limb prosthetic users (≥18 years, prosthesis ≥6 months) using structured questionnaires covering sociodemographic data, clinical characteristics, patient satisfaction (SAT-PRO/PEQ/TAPES), and quality of life (EQ-5D-5L and SWLS).

**Primary Outcomes:**
- SWLS (Satisfaction With Life Scale) — 5 to 35 score range
- Prosthetic satisfaction — SAT-PRO / PEQ / TAPES adapted interview

**Secondary Outcomes:**
- WHOQOL-BREF domain scores
- EQ-5D-5L index score

**Key Significance:** One of very few prosthetic outcomes studies conducted in a humanitarian/crisis setting. Similar methodology could be applied to rural U.S.Access gaps.

## Trial 2: ASCENT K2 (NCT04784429)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Title** | Assessing Outcomes With Microprocessor Knee Utilization in a K2 Population |
| **Acronym** | ASCENT K2 |
| **Status** | ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING |
| **Sponsor** | Otto Bock Healthcare Products GmbH (INDUSTRY) |
| **Collaborators** | Hanger Clinic: Prosthetics & Orthotics (OTHER) |
| **Start Date** | 2021-06-11 (ACTUAL) |
| **Estimated Completion** | 2026-12-31 (ESTIMATED) |
| **Enrollment** | 107 (ACTUAL) |
| **Type** | INTERVENTIONAL — Randomized Controlled Trial |
| **Phase** | NA (Device study) |
| **Condition** | Lower Limb Amputation Above Knee, Congenital Amputation, Lower Limb Amputation Knee |
| **Keywords** | Prosthetic knee, lower limb amputation, microprocessor controlled knee, K2 |

**Brief Summary:**
Two-phased RCT comparing microprocessor controlled knee prostheses (MPK: Kenevo or C-Leg 4) with non-microprocessor controlled knee prostheses (NMPK) in patients with transfemoral/knee disarticulation amputation categorized as K2 ambulators (Limited Community Ambulators per Medicare K-Level).

**Phase 1 (12 months):** Fear/anxiety of falling reduction, health-related QoL, participation in society
**Phase 2 (12mo–5yr):** Long-term effect including reduced morbidity

**Primary Outcomes:**
- FFABQ (Fear of Falling Avoidance Behavior Questionnaire) — 14 items, 5-point scale, score 0–56

**Secondary Outcomes:**
- PROMIS-29 (7 health domains → T-scores 50±10)
- PROMIS-APSRA (social roles/activities participation)
- 12-month fall count from bi-weekly fall journal
- Average daily step counts (triaxial accelerometry)
- FFABQ at 3 and 5 years
- PM-time activity measures

**Key Significance:** Landmark RCT on MPK vs conventional knees. Hanger Clinic collaboration means results could directly influence clinical practice standards for K2 patients.

## Trial 3: ProFit (NCT02540681)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Title** | Prosthetic Fit Assessment in Transtibial Amputees Secondary to Trauma |
| **Acronym** | ProFit |
| **Status** | COMPLETED |
| **Sponsor** | Major Extremity Trauma Research Consortium (OTHER) |
| **Collaborators** | University of California, San Francisco (OTHER) |
| **Start Date** | 2015-01 |
| **Completion** | 2021-01 (ACTUAL) |
| **Enrollment** | 33 (ACTUAL) |
| **Type** | OBSERVATIONAL — Prospective Cohort Validation |
| **Oversight** | DMC (Data Monitoring Committee) present |
| **Condition** | Traumatic Amputation of Lower Extremity |
| **Flags** | isFdaRegulatedDevice=true |

**Brief Summary:**
Validation study of the ProFit — a 42-item assessment tool for prosthetic fit and alignment among transtibial amputees. Developed by a panel of certified orthotist prosthetists (CPOs) in collaboration with orthopaedic trauma investigators. Uses x-ray, photo, and video assessment.

**Three Aims:**
1. **Reliability:** Inter-rater and internal consistency of ProFit
2. **Validity:** Construct validity (correlation with Brief Pain Inventory, Short Musculoskeletal Functional Assessment, Orthotics and Prosthetics User's Survey)
3. **Responsiveness:** Repeated measurements at 6 and 18 months; develop shorter version

**Sites:** Up to 35 centers (24 Core METRC, 4 MTFs, 7 satellite). Mississippi site at University of Mississippi Medical Center (Jackson, MS).

**Key Significance:** First validated prosthetic fit assessment tool. Critical for standardizing quality of fitting across prosthetists and clinics — directly relevant to access gaps where users may see different providers.

## Trial 5: Access/Equity Gap

**No dedicated access/equity-focused clinical trials** were identified among the 644 prosthetic-related studies.

This is itself a critical finding. The literature overwhelmingly focuses on:
- Device performance (microprocessor knees, osseointegration, bionics)
- Surgical outcomes (fitting, revision)
- Diagnostic tools (PJI detection)
- QoL and satisfaction metrics

But virtually **none** address:
- Geographic access barriers
- Rural vs. urban disparities
- Cost/insurance coverage gaps
- Wait times for prosthetic services
- Mobile/outreach care models
- Workforce shortages in O&P

**This gap represents both a problem and an opportunity for the Prosthetic Access Atlas.**
