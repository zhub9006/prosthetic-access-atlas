## Notable Active & Recent Trials

### 1. MIRA — Myoelectric Implantable Recording Array (NCT05768802)
- **Status**: RECRUITING
- **Sponsor**: University of Pittsburgh / Drs. Boninger & Gaunt
- **Collaborators**: Ripple Therapeutics Corporation, NINDS (NIH)
- **Intervention**: Implantable intramuscular EMG array (MIRA) for prosthetic hand control
- **Population**: Transradial amputation participants
- **Enrollment**: 5 (estimated)
- **Phase**: N/A (Device Feasibility)
- **FDA Regulated Device**: Yes (unapproved)
- **Key Innovation**: 8 implanted electrodes enabling simultaneous multi-DOF control; eliminates percutaneous connections; telerehabilitation-enabled
- **Start**: Jan 2026 | Completion: ~Dec 2030
- **Link**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT05768802

### 2. PROINGA — Prosthetic Satisfaction & Quality of Life Among Lower Limb Users in Gaza (NCT07519746)
- **Status**: COMPLETED
- **Sponsor**: Yeditepe University / Al-Azhar Gaza
- **Population**: Lower limb amputees in conflict zone
- **Enrollment**: 128
- **Instruments**: SAT-PRO, PEQ, TAPES, EQ-5D-5L, SWLS
- **Key Finding**: Prosthetic satisfaction heavily influenced by access barriers and conflict-related device damage
- **Link**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07519746

### 3. MPK-K2 Trial — Microprocessor Knee for K2-Level Ambulators (NCT06498245)
- **Status**: RECRUITING
- **Design**: Randomized controlled trial
- **Population**: K2-level community ambulators
- **Intervention**: Microprocessor prosthetic knee vs. conventional knee
- **Significance**: Directly compares advanced vs. standard prosthetic technology
- **Link**: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06498245

## Key Trends & Observations

1. **Misalignment between trials and need**: 42% of studies are COMPLETED while only 17.5% are actively RECRUITING — innovation is outpacing deployment.
2. **Device feasibility dominates**: 52.5% of studies are N/A-phase (device feasibility/observational), suggesting most prosthetic research stays in early-stage experimentation rather than comparative effectiveness.
3. **U.S. dominates geographically**: Nearly all studies involve US sites, yet the access gap maps show these very regions (Appalachia, Delta) lack the infrastructure to benefit from these innovations.
4. **Industry vs. Academic divide**: 76% of studies are academic/other sponsored, while only 17% are industry-sponsored — commercial translation of findings remains weak.
5. **Pediatric/prosthetic gap**: Very few studies specifically target pediatric prosthetics or growing-residual-limb management.
6. **Long follow-up is rare**: Most studies plan ≤12 month follow-up, but prosthetics need multi-year data for socket fit, skin integrity, and device durability.
