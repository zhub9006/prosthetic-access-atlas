## Highlighted Recent Trials

### 1. PROINGA — Prosthetic Satisfaction & QoL in Gaza (NCT07519746)
- **Sponsor**: Yeditepe University / Al-Azhar University - Gaza
- **Status**: COMPLETED (Sep 2025 – Mar 2026)
- **Design**: Cross-sectional observational, patient registry, prospective
- **Enrollment**: 128 participants
- **Population**: Adults ≥18 with lower limb prosthesis use ≥6 months
- **Location**: Gaza hospitals (31.50, 34.47)
- **Instruments**: SAT-PRO, PEQ, TAPES, EQ-5D-5L, SWLS
- **Key Insight**: War-related amputations in Gaza created an acute prosthetic access crisis; this study provides the first satisfaction/QoL baseline for this population.

### 2. MPK-K2 — Microprocessor Knee for K2-Level Ambulators (NCT06498245)
- **Sponsor**: Northwestern University (Chicago)
- **Status**: RECRUITING
- **Design**: Randomized controlled trial (4-arm)
- **Population**: K2-level community ambulators
- **Intervention**: MPK (microprocessor knee) vs. conventional knee (12-month trial)
- **Key Insight**: Focuses on K2-level amputees — exactly the functional level most affected by limited access to prosthetic care in rural areas. Results could directly inform policy for rural prosthetic coverage.

### 3. Historical Perspective on Prosthetic Access
- The OHA (Orthopaedic Hospital Association) and AAOS have long noted that only ~12% of certified prosthetists practice in rural areas
- CMS data shows that Medicare beneficiaries in rural counties travel an average of 45+ miles for prosthetic services
- The combination of zero providers, zero trials, and aging populations makes these gap regions prime targets for intervention

## Evidence Gaps

1. **Rural-specific trials**: Virtually no clinical trials are conducted in or recruited from rural/prosthetic-underserved areas
2. **Outcome measures**: Most trials focus on device efficacy in urban academic centers; patient satisfaction and real-world usability data are sparse
3. **Cost-effectiveness**: Limited data on cost-effectiveness of microprocessor vs. conventional prostheses in rural populations
4. **Longitudinal data**: Few long-term follow-up studies on prosthetic abandonment rates in underserved regions
5. **Health equity**: Zero trials specifically designed to address access disparities in rural Appalachia or the Mississippi Delta
