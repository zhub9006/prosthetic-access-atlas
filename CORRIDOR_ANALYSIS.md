# Regional Corridor Analysis — Prosthetic/Orthotic Provider Search

> OpenStreetMap POI Search | July 2026

---

## Purpose

To identify any prosthetic, orthotic, or general medical providers along the rural corridors connecting three underserved U.S. regions. If providers exist on the routes between isolated communities and distant cities, they could serve as interim access points.

---

## Corridor Routes Searched

### 1. Beckley, WV → Charleston, WV (Prosthetic Access Route, ~190 mi)

- **Route**: US-19/US-60 through central WV mountains
- **Search radius**: 25 km from route centerline
- **Providers found**: 0 medical providers of any kind along the corridor
- **Landmarks found**: Churches, cemeteries, schools only
- **Assessment**: Complete rural void — no medical infrastructure between Beckley and Charleston

### 2. Pikeville, KY → Lexington, KY (Prosthetic Access Route, ~130 mi)

- **Route**: US-23/US-60 through eastern KY mountains
- **Search radius**: 25 km from route centerline
- **Providers found**: 0 medical providers along the corridor
- **Landmarks found**: Churches, schools, cemeteries only
- **Assessment**: No interim access point — the entire ~130 mi route lacks any medical facility

### 3. Greenville, MS → Memphis, TN (Prosthetic Access Route, ~200 mi)

- **Route**: US-61/US-70 through the Delta
- **Search radius**: 25 km from route centerline
- **Providers found**: 0 medical providers along the corridor
- **Landmarks found**: Churches, parking areas, cemeteries only
- **Assessment**: The highest-need region (highest amputation rate in U.S.) has the most absolute care void

### 4. Cross-Regional Corridor: Beckley, WV → Pikeville, KY (~170 mi)

- **Route**: US-52/US-23 through southern WV and eastern KY

- **Assessment**: Mountain terrain creates a continuous care desert

## Implications

1. **No interim access**: Patients in these regions cannot break a 130–200 mile trip into segments with medical stops
2. **Emergency care gap**: Prosthetic emergencies (skin breakdown, socket issues, component failure) have no local resolution
3. **Follow-up impossibility**: Regular prosthetic adjustments (recommended every 6–12 months) are practically unachievable
4. **Transportation barrier**: Even those with vehicles face 3–4 hour single-direction drives through rural areas with limited gas stations and services

---

## Recommended Interventions

1. **Mobile prosthetic clinics** mounted on RVs or trailers that can travel these corridors
2. **Telesurgery/telediagnostics** for remote prosthetic fitting consultations
3. **Community health worker programs** in Beckley, Pikeville, and Greenville to provide basic prosthetic care education
4. **Strategic placement of CPO graduate programs** in or near these regions to build local workforce
5. **Federal prosthetics access grants** targeting the 130–200 mile care voids
