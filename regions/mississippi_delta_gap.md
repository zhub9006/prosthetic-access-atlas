# Mississippi Delta: Prosthetic/Orthotic Access Desert

## Geography

The Mississippi Delta region of northwestern Mississippi is one of the most underserved regions in the United States. The analysis focuses on two anchor cities:

- **Clarksdale, MS** (34.20, -90.57) — Coahoma County seat, population ~14,000
- **Greenwood, MS** (33.52, -90.18) — Leflore County seat, population ~12,000

## Findings

### No healthcare facilities of any kind within 100 km

The most striking finding is the **complete absence of listed healthcare amenities** (hospitals, clinics, doctors, pharmacies, or physiotherapy) within the OSM dataset for the broader Delta. Only churches, cemeteries, schools, and commercial retail (Dollar General, convenience stores) appear.

### Dimensions of the Crisis

1. **Mississippi has the highest rate of diabetes in the nation** → highest amputation rate
2. **Predominantly Black population** — structural healthcare access disparities compound the geographic gap
3. **Medicaid expansion gap** — MS did not expand Medicaid; coverage barriers compound access barriers
4. **Food desert + medically underserved** — simultaneous lack of multiple care types
5. **Few CPOs in entire state** — MS has fewer than 20 licensed CPOs for a population of ~3 million

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Mobile CPO clinic deployments** — at least 2 mobile units rotating between Clarksdale & Greenwood
2. **Tele-rehab + measurement hubs** — University of Mississippi Medical Center telehealth partnerships
3. **Travel subsidy programs** — Medicaid/Medicare waiver for travel to CPO care
4. **Train local community health workers** — prosthetic care navigation
5. **VA partnership expansion** — utilize Memphis VA for Delta amputee veterans
