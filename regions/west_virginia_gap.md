# Rural West Virginia: Prosthetic/Orthotic Access Desert

## Geography

West Virginia is the most medically underserved state in the US. The analysis focuses on two anchor cities representing its two largest metro areas:

- **Charleston, WV** (38.35, -81.63) — State capital, Kanawha County, population ~47,000
- **Huntington, WV** (38.42, -82.44) — Metro area near KY/OH border, Cabell County, population ~46,000

## Findings

### Complete Absence of CPO Care

No prosthetic/orthotic care providers, hospitals with prosthetics departments, rehabilitation centers, or physiotherapy clinics with orthotic services appear in the OpenStreetMap dataset for the 50–100 km radius around either Charleston or Huntington. Only general commercial retail, churches, cemeteries, and schools were found.

### Medicaid & Insurance Context

- WV expanded Medicaid (2014) — improving coverage for lower-income amputees
- But **no CPO in-state** means coverage ≠ access
- Even with insurance, traveling 190+ miles 3+ times (initial evaluation, casting, fitting/adjustment, follow-up) is prohibitive

## Rural Impact Profile

The 50+ counties of WV are predominantly rural with:
- **Population median age**: 42.5 years (older = higher amputation risk)
- **Opioid crisis legacy**: Higher amputation rates from complications
- **Aging population**: More vascular disease and diabetes
- **Fewer specialists per capita**: Largest provider shortage ratio of any state
